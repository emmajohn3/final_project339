Hungary

Day 1

On July 11th, we flew from Detroit, Michigan to Frankfurt, Germany for our layover. Originally, we were supposed to fly to Amsterdam, but our change in flight got us an upgrade to Delta One seats! They were super spacious and we were able to lay all the way back in our seats. Then, we flew from Frankfurt to Budapest, Hungary. Jack and I sat in the very back row of the plane. Jack’s luggage got lost but was returned the next day, thankfully!

Detroit, Michigan

Budapest, Hungary

We arrived at our hotel, the Intercontinental Budapest, and rested for a short while because we were tired from all the travel and the time change. Afterwards, we ate dinner at the hotel restaurant. We ordered a charcuterie board with hummus, which ended up being Jack’s favorite meal of the trip.

At night, we took a night cruise down the Danube River. It was amazing to see Budapest at night, and the Parliament Building was incredible with its lights on.

Day 2

We woke up at 6:45 for our morning tour of the Parliament building. Since it was early, we had the front of the building all to ourselves for pictures. We took an audio guided tour that walked us through the building and told us about its history.

After, we took a trolley to Castle Hill. We used a golf cart tour to take us around. We visited Fisherman’s Bastion, Old Town, and Buda Castle. Fisherman’s Bastion had incredible views of the city and the Danube River.

Later, we visited St. Stephen’s Basilica and got dinner and drinks. At dinner, there was a band that played My Way by Frank Sinatra for Aunt Lisa.

Gyor, Hungary

In the morning, we drove from Budapest to Gyor, Hungary. The city had many pastel buildings and large churches. It even had fountains that you could drink from!

Bratislava, Slovakia

Then, we drove to Bratislava, Slovakia. We visited the Blue Church, where everything in it was blue. We walked around the Old Town, did some shopping, and had lunch.
